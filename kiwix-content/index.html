Offline Content Guide

Kiwix (already installed) lets you read Wikipedia and other reference content with no internet connection at all — but the content itself has to be downloaded once, while you have real bandwidth. This page is a short, curated list of what's actually worth downloading, instead of Kiwix's own full catalog (over a thousand entries, mostly unrelated wikis, docs, and talks).

These are large files (0.6–4.2GB each). Download them on a real broadband connection — a school, library, or office network — not mobile data, and not while on a metered/capped connection. Once downloaded, they work forever with no connection at all.

Already installed: Vikidia (Kids' Encyclopedia) 103 MB

A Wikipedia-style encyclopedia written specifically for children (ages 8–13) — simpler language, curated for classroom use. Small enough that it ships with every install, no download needed at all. Look for Vikidia (Kids' Encyclopedia) in the applications menu.

How to use these

Click a "Download" button below (opens in Firefox, already installed). Once it finishes downloading, open Kiwix from the applications menu, choose File → Open, and select the downloaded .zim file. Kiwix will remember it from then on.

Health

MedlinePlus 1.8 GB

Consumer health information from the U.S. National Library of Medicine — conditions, medications, symptoms, and general health topics, written for a general (non-medical-professional) audience.

Download

Not Pacific-specific — a well-regarded general health reference, not local health-service info (see the Services Directory app for that).

General knowledge

Wikipedia — Vital Articles (0.8, no images) 2.2 GB

Wikipedia's own curated selection of its highest-importance, highest-quality articles (around 50,000) across every subject — not the full 100GB+ encyclopedia, but a genuinely useful core reference rather than a random or shallow subset.

Download

Education

Project Gutenberg — Education collection 600 MB

Public-domain books on education and teaching from Project Gutenberg's library.

Download

Same honest caveat as the agriculture collection below: older, out-of-copyright books, not a modern curriculum.

Agriculture

Project Gutenberg — Agriculture collection 4.2 GB

Public-domain books on farming and agriculture from Project Gutenberg's library.

Download

Honest caveat: this is a collection of older, out-of-copyright books, not modern agricultural extension material. It's the best option available in Kiwix's public library for this topic at the time this was written — genuinely current, Pacific-relevant farming guidance would be a good thing for a future contributor to source and package separately.

Looking for disaster-preparedness info?

Pacific Linux's own Disaster Readiness app (applications menu) already covers cyclone/tsunami/earthquake safety and emergency contacts for Vanuatu and Fiji, sourced from official government channels — that's a better starting point than Kiwix's generic "prepper" content, which is written for a different (US-centric, survivalist) audience and isn't Pacific-specific.
